Sharing What I've Learned

Sales & GTM - A Glimpse

Still learning every day, but here are some patterns and frameworks I've picked up along the way. Click any stage to expand.

⚙️ Engine 1 - The Direct Sales Machine
Stage 1 - Precision Prospecting ▾

The spray-and-pray era is dead. Enterprise prospecting is now an intelligence operation. Build your ICP on technographic fit, use intent data to time outreach, and map the org chart before you touch the phone.


Role-Based Personalisation: New executive (0–6 months): message around quick wins and risk mitigation. Tenured executive: protecting their legacy. Functional leader: day-to-day friction and making their number. End user: the specific frustration they live every single day.


Channels that work: LinkedIn (warm before you cold, comment for 2 weeks first), Cold Email (one idea per email, subject under 5 words), Cold Call (lead with a hypothesis, not a pitch), Loom video (converts 3–4× better than text).

Want a prospecting framework built for your specific ICP and market? I work with early-stage GTM teams on building surgical outbound engines.

in ✉
Stage 2 - Discovery: ANOT Framework + Iceberg Model ▾

ANOT: Appreciation → News (reference something specific) → Objectives (build a mutual agenda) → Time (protect it).


The Iceberg Model: Surface: "Our team wastes time on manual reporting." Root cause: "The CFO doesn't trust the numbers." Cost of inaction: "We missed a board presentation and now the CFO is questioning the entire tech stack."


Questions that work: "Walk me through what a bad week looks like." / "What have you already tried - and why didn't it stick?" / "If this problem is still here in 18 months, what does that mean for you personally?"


End every discovery call with a Mutual Action Plan (MAP). This single habit separates sellers who control their pipeline from those who are controlled by it.

Stage 3 - The Demo: Solution vs. Pain ▾

Never demo a feature that doesn't solve a previously stated pain. Three acts: Before State (their words, their pain) → Transition Moment ("What if that three-day problem took twenty minutes?") → After State (only the relevant workflow).


The demo close: Never end with "Any questions?" End with "Based on what you've seen today, does this solve the problem we discussed?" Then stop talking. The silence belongs to them.

Stage 4 - Proof of Concept: The Controlled Experiment ▾

A PoC is not "let them poke around." It's a controlled experiment with a predetermined hypothesis and pre-agreed success metric. Run a Best-Case Scenario session upfront. Weekly check-ins are non-negotiable. Present their own data back to them at the readout.

Stage 5 - Value Engineering & Cost of Inaction ▾

Not a slide deck - a collaborative exercise the customer builds with you. Quantify current state pain → model future state gain → calculate the cost of inaction (the most powerful number in the room) → show the payback period. A 6–9 month payback removes most CFO objections at a stroke.

I run Value Engineering workshops for GTM teams and founders building commercial narratives for enterprise sales or investor conversations.

in ✉
Stage 6 - Negotiation & Final Closing ▾

Expand the pie before you split it. Tactical empathy: Labelling ("It seems like the budget timing is creating pressure..."), Mirroring (repeat last 2–3 words, then wait), Accusation Audit (address their objections before they raise them). Create urgency through their own business calendar - not artificial pressure.

🤝 Engine 2 - The Strategic Partnership Machine
Phase 1 - Partner Identification & Tiering ▾

Tier 1 - GSIs: Accenture, Deloitte, KPMG, TCS, Infosys. Deep integration, joint GTM, C-suite access in Fortune 500.

Tier 2 - Regional SIs: Mid-size firms with deep industry specialisation. Faster to activate, closer to the client's day-to-day.

Tier 3 - ISV Partners: Technical stickiness and expanded use cases.


Score partners on: ICP alignment, executive sponsorship, practice maturity, 12-month revenue potential, and competitive risk.

Phase 2–3 - Onboarding & Centre of Excellence ▾

The most underinvested phase. Don't just sign an agreement and send credentials - run a structured Alliance Kickoff with a "first deal in 90 days" challenge. The CoE is not a training programme. It's a business-building engine that makes your partners commercially dangerous without you in the room.

I've built partner enablement frameworks from initial tiering through CoE design and joint GTM planning.

in ✉
Phase 4–5 - Partner Marketing & Co-Selling ▾

The rule of partner marketing: attach yourself to their momentum, then amplify it. Use Crossbeam for account mapping - identify where your partner owns the account (warm intro opportunity), where you're in and they're not (value-add opportunity), and where both are in but misaligned (immediate alignment required).

Phase 6 - Partner Success: Post-Sale Alignment ▾

The most neglected phase - and the one that determines whether the partner brings you the next deal. Joint value realisation reports at 3 and 6 months. The expansion motion: a successful implementation is your best lead generation asset. The cost of the next sale is a fraction of the first.

Most partnerships stall because the post-sale motion isn't designed before the deal closes. If you're building a partner success playbook, let's talk.

Deal Guardrails - The Red Flag Stress Tests

🚩 Champion vs. Coach Test

Ask your champion to introduce you to the Economic Buyer. If they hesitate, they are a Coach - friendly, but powerless. A true Champion pulls you upward into the organisation.

🚩 Silence After the Demo

If the prospect stops asking "how does this work?" and starts asking "what happens if we don't do this?" - you're winning. Silence after a great demo means someone has an unshared objection.

🚩 The Procurement Surprise

If the first time you hear from Legal is the week the deal should close, your deal is at risk. Introduce yourself to procurement early - not as an adversary at the finish line.

🚩 The Internal Salesperson Check

Can your champion explain your Value Engineering deck in their own words? If they can't articulate ROI and cost of inaction, they cannot sell it to the CFO when you're not in the room.

🚩 The Single-Threaded Deal

One contact = zero deals, just a conversation. Enterprise deals require multi-threading. If your champion leaves the company, your deal should not die with them.

🚩 The Paper Process Surprise

Every large enterprise has an internal approval process above a certain spend threshold. Map it in your first commercial conversation, not the week you're trying to close.

These guardrails come from real deals - some won, some lost, all instructive. Running a live enterprise deal and want a second opinion?

The 10/10 Shelf

These books will help anyone get started in sales.

01
Never Split the Difference - Chris Voss

Negotiation is not a logic problem - it's an emotional one. Tactical empathy, labelling, and the counterintuitive power of "no." The mirroring technique alone has saved deals that logic and spreadsheets could not.

Best for: Anyone in enterprise sales, alliances, or commercial leadership
02
The Challenger Sale - Dixon & Adamson

The best performers don't lead with relationships - they lead with insight. They teach customers something new about their own business. This is the book that made "consultative selling" mean something specific.

Best for: AEs moving from transactional to strategic selling
03
SPIN Selling - Neil Rackham

In complex B2B sales, the quality of your questions matters infinitely more than the quality of your pitch. Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff is a diagnostic model, not a script.

Best for: Anyone in a discovery-heavy sales cycle
04
Founding Sales - Pete Kazanjy

The most underrated sales book ever written. Operational, honest, written for builders rather than theorists. The book someone should give you on your first day in SaaS, but almost never does.

Best for: Founders, early-stage AEs, career switchers entering B2B SaaS
05
Gap Selling - Keenan

The gap between a prospect's current state and desired future state is where your value lives. If you can't define that gap with precision, you cannot close with confidence.

Best for: AEs sharpening discovery and value articulation in competitive deals
06
Fanatical Prospecting - Jeb Blount

Pipeline cures everything. The most elegant sales process in the world is worthless without enough top-of-funnel activity. The discomfort of accepting that relentless prospecting is non-negotiable is the point.

Best for: SDRs, BDRs, and any AE whose pipeline is thinner than their comfort admits

FoodBridge

INNOVA Europe 2026 · Top 2 Finalist · Team Lead

The Problem

60 million tonnes of EU food waste annually while 40 million EU citizens cannot afford a quality meal every second day. The EU Waste Framework Directive (2025) mandates 30% restaurant food waste reduction by 2030 - binding, enforceable, with fines up to €500,000. Existing solutions start at €12–18K/year, pricing out the 1.5 million SME restaurants the law targets.

The Founding Insight

The compliance burden is the opportunity. Reframed the question from "how do we convince restaurants to care?" to "how do we give them the cheapest, most friction-free path to compliance?"

Business Model

  • Transaction commission: €0.80–1.20 per surplus bag moved
  • Consumer surplus marketplace: €3–5 bags via app
  • Compliance SaaS: €89–149/venue/month
  • Data licensing (Year 2+): EU's most granular SME food waste dataset

The Vision

Year 5 projection: 100,000 venues across the EU, €45M subscription ARR, €62M consumer marketplace GMV annually.

What I Learned

Impact and revenue need to share the same sentence. Legitimacy is granular - one wrong number and a judge discounts everything else. Every stat in our deck was traced to a primary EU institutional source.

Nexus Irish Whiskey

ISCG × Accenture Competition · International Expansion Strategy

The Core Insight

The community isn't a marketing channel - it's the competitive moat itself. Jameson has an €80M+ marketing budget. Nexus cannot win that game. We reframed the expansion question: instead of "which market has the most whiskey drinkers," we asked "which market has the most activatable community?"

Nexus OS (explained simply)

The brand's operating system - three layers: a zero-party data intelligence backbone, the Nexus App as a co-ownership platform (not a punch card), and the Batch Passport. The psychological difference between "I have 450 reward points" and "I'm a Brand Council member who voted on this cask" is enormous.

The Batch Passport

A QR code on every bottle. Scan it: full provenance story - grain harvest, distillation run, community vote that determined the finish, who named the batch, how many bottles produced. Every sip traceable to a community decision.

What I Learned

In category-challenger brands built on community, the brand strategy should be the business strategy. When those two documents are separate, something has gone wrong in the thinking.

SOS Cookies

Strategic Expansion & Scaling · Dublin, Ireland

The Brief

Identify precisely where, when, and how to strategically scale SOS Cookies across Ireland through kiosks, pop-ups, and permanent retail. Our team audited production capacity, staffing needs, and logistics.

The Core Insight

Growth was bottlenecked by a production limit of 2,400 cookies daily. Strategy shifted from rapid expansion to growing smart - prioritising high-footfall Dublin locations to maximise current capacity before committing to major capital expenditure.

The Strategy

Benchmarked against competitors including The Rolling Donut. Proposed product diversification: character cookies for kids, mini-bites for health-conscious adults. A smart marketing layer using AI tools for viral content generation.

The Roadmap (2025-2028)

Short-term: Liffey Valley, Tallaght, UCD locations. Medium-term: Market entry into Cork and London. 2026 cap: 3-4 new locations max to protect quality and brand consistency.

What I Learned

Operational structure is the prerequisite for creative innovation. You can have the most viral brand in the city, but without a documented supply chain and a Founder-to-Manager mindset, you cannot scale.
